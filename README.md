<!-- Questions and Answers -->
<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">1. Role of control group in an experiment:</div>
  <div class="answer">The control group is used to compare results and see the effect of the experiment.</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">2. Formula for relative velocity (same direction):</div>
  <div class="answer">v<sub>relative</sub> = v₁ - v₂ (Same direction → subtract velocities)</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">3. Function of calyx in the flower:</div>
  <div class="answer">The calyx protects the flower bud before it opens.</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">4. Organic acid:</div>
  <div class="answer">Organic acids contain carbon in their molecules.</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">5. Strength comparison (organic vs chemical acids):</div>
  <div class="answer">Organic acids are weaker than chemical (inorganic) acids.</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">6. Example of edible acid:</div>
  <div class="answer">Citric acid (in lemon) or acetic acid (in vinegar)</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">7. Effect of acid on indicators:</div>
  <div class="answer">Blue litmus → Red, Methyl orange → Red</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">8. One way to minimize acid rain:</div>
  <div class="answer">Plant more trees.</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">9. Type of water if soap produces lather easily:</div>
  <div class="answer">Soft water</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">10. Provide any two examples of experimental work:</div>
  <div class="answer">1. Growing plants in sunlight<br>2. Acid reacting with metal</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">11. Two features of an ISP:</div>
  <div class="answer">1. Provides internet connection to users<br>2. Offers email, web hosting, and other online services</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">12. Why metabolism occurs in the cytoplasm:</div>
  <div class="answer">It contains all the enzymes and substances needed for chemical reactions.</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">13. Enzymes mean:</div>
  <div class="answer">Proteins that speed up chemical reactions without being used up.</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">14. In-situ conservation means:</div>
  <div class="answer">Protecting plants or animals in their natural habitat.</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">15. Two examples of in-situ conservation:</div>
  <div class="answer">1. National parks (e.g., Chitwan)<br>2. Wildlife sanctuaries (e.g., Bardiya)</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">16. Wildlife sanctuary means:</div>
  <div class="answer">An area where wild animals are protected and allowed to live freely.</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">17. Why are non-stick vessel and pressure cooker handles made of plastic-like materials?</div>
  <div class="answer">Because plastic does not conduct heat, so handles stay cool and safe to touch.</div>
</div>

<div class="qa">
  <div class="question">18. Why comets gradually lose their materials when revolving around the Sun many times?</div>
  <div class="answer">The Sun’s heat causes their ice and dust to evaporate, forming a tail and gradually losing material.</div>
</div>
